Research Problem Statement

Despite rapid fitness culture growth, retailers are unable to differentiate between performance-driven and wellness-oriented consumers effectively.

The lack of segment-specific buying triggers, trust factors, and price sensitivity awareness leads to ineffective marketing and suboptimal sales in Tier-2 cities.

This research directly addresses the gap by mapping consumer behaviour across both segments at scale.

Activity Profile by Consumer Type
💪 FITNESS-ORIENTED — 30 respondents

Weight training, running, gym-based activities. 82% motivated by muscle gain. High supplement usage intent and willingness to pay.

🧘 WELLNESS-ORIENTED — 41 respondents

Yoga, jogs, recreational sports. Doctor-driven adoption (63%). More price-sensitive and brand-conscious than fitness consumers.

🤷 NEITHER — 9 respondents

Casual / non-committed exercisers. Low gym frequency. Supplement skepticism most pronounced in this group.

Strategic Research Findings
01
Distinct Buying Triggers
Fitness consumers are triggered by muscle gain (82%) while wellness consumers respond to doctor recommendations (63%). One-size marketing fails both.
SEGMENTATION
02
Price Sensitivity Gap
Wellness consumers are significantly more price-sensitive (68%) vs fitness consumers (46%). Premium pricing works for fitness; value bundles win wellness.
PRICING
03
Brand Over Online Reviews
Product brand trust (41%) crushes online reviews (9%). Certification & authenticity matter far more than influencer marketing.
TRUST
04
Tier-2 Concentration Opportunity
26 supplement stores in Raipur = 33% of all Chhattisgarh stores. Yet 31.25% of respondents are Tier-2, representing an underserved, high-growth segment.
GEOGRAPHY
05
22% Awareness Gap
Despite 42% high awareness, 22% have low awareness of supplement benefits. Education-led content marketing is a major untapped growth lever.
AWARENESS
06
Outdoor Sports Dominate
Badminton (38), Cricket (28) and Running (23) far outpace gym activities. Brands should invest in sports sponsorship & outdoor activation not just gym placements.
